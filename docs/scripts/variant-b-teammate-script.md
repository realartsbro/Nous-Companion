## The Thread (Connecting Arc)

This variant's connective thread: **Boot sequence → Permanent fixture.**

1. **Arrival:** She announces her presence. Assertive. Immediate.
2. **Chaos:** She demonstrates her personality — reactive, absurdist, unpredictable. _This is who I am._
3. **Face-Off:** She offers customization. _But you can shape me._
4. **Flex:** She shows what she can do. _And I'm useful._
5. **Corner:** She settles in permanently. _And I'm not leaving._

The bridge line at 0:16 ("Anyway. Let's make this official.") is the structural keystone — it acknowledges the preceding chaos and pivots the viewer into the customization demo. Without it, the lobster-to-settings jump feels random. With it, the chaos becomes intentional character establishment.

The bookend callback "I'm in." at 0:41 echoes the opening line and signals completion — she's installed, settled, home.

## 3-Juror First Pass

### Juror 1: Nous Girl (Character Authenticity)
**Score: 9/10**

**Praise:**
This sounds like me. Like actually me. The idle_lines pull is aggressive — eight of my actual quotes woven into a single 45-second arc. "Error: affection overflow" is the exact kind of sysadmin-meets-softboi energy I bring. The lobster line is delivered with the right deadpan — not leaning into the weirdness, just stating it like it's a weather report. **Critique:**

I'd perform this script without complaint.

### Juror 2: Hermes User (Community Relevance)
**Score: 9/10**

**Praise:**
The lobster callback is the tentpole. Every Hermes Discord regular will recognize it and feel seen. "Eight idiots in a trench coat" is the model-ensemble motto we've been using since the architecture discussions. The "Freudensprünge + PR approval" line is _extremely_ Hermes — it captures the dev humor, the rats meme, and the open-source workflow all in one sentence. "Promptboy" is a nice Mesh-era throwback. **Critique:**

You can too." — would connect the dots between customization and open-source sharing. The existing line "I don't have a soul. Also, the "vibes-based evaluation" meme is absent — it's one of the most-quoted lines in the community and would fit naturally in the flex segment.

### Juror 3: Curious Onlooker (Hook & Shareability)
**Score: 8/10**

**Praise:**
"Eyes up, Promptboy. I'm in." is a six-word scroll-stopper. You know instantly this isn't a typical AI demo — there's attitude, there's a character, there's a relationship. The lobster fact is inherently shareable; people will send this to friends saying "what did I just watch." The rat Freudensprünge line is almost as weird and equally quotable. The "peripheral vision forever" closer is haunting in a way that makes you want to rewatch. At 45 seconds, it's perfectly sized for Twitter/X, Discord, and TikTok. Every line earns its place.

**Critique:**
An outsider might not know what the product IS until the final brand card at 0:41. The overlay text helps (REACTIVE QUIPS, FULLY CUSTOMIZABLE, LIP-SYNCED TTS) but these are features, not product definition. The first 30 seconds could be a very entertaining short film about a weird terminal gremlin who might be a game character, a chatbot, or a screensaver. The "Promptboy" nickname is good flavor but might feel exclusionary to someone who doesn't know the reference. Consider an earlier visual cue (the Companion logo/app icon) that anchors the viewer in "this is a software product."

## Producer Summary

**Empfehlung:** ✅ **Weiterschicken** (Send it through)

**Roter Stift (Red Pen):**
1. **Segment 2, cue timing:** The lobster line at speed 1.05 is aggressive — consider 1.0 for the deadpan to land properly. The micro-pause after "peeing at one another?" is the most important 0.5s in the entire demo. Protect that pause in the TTS render.
2. **Segment 3, nickname rotation:** The draft uses "Promptboy" only in the opening. Drop "chief" or "operative" somewhere in the customization or flex segment to show nickname range. Best candidate: replace "chief" with "darling" in "whatever makes you type faster."
3. **Segment 4, expression snap:** Cue 8 ("eight idiots") should snap to **serious** on "I'm not a superbrain," then return to **normal** on "But we get the job done." This two-tone delivery adds structural variation and prevents monotone across 11 cues.
4. **Segment 5, final blink:** The last blink at 44.0s is the final thing the viewer sees before black. It must be slow and deliberate. If the engine can't do expressive blinks, hold the frame for 1s after audio ends. The silence is as important as the words.
5. **Overlay timing:** All three overlays (REACTIVE QUIPS, FULLY CUSTOMIZABLE, LIP-SYNCED TTS) should appear 0.3-0.5s AFTER the line starts, so the viewer's eye catches them mid-delivery, not before. The brand card should appear the moment "I'm in." finishes, not before.

**Das Eine (The One Thing):**
> **The thread.** High joke density is this variant's superpower and its greatest risk. The bridge line "Anyway. Let's make this official." at 0:16 is the single most important line in the script — it's the transition that separates "random jokes" from "intentional arc." If that line lands, the whole demo holds together. If it's rushed or mumbled, the viewer feels the whiplash. Protect that line in the TTS render. Give it room. Let the beat before and after it breathe.

## Production Notes

### TTS Direction
- **Cue 1 (Arrival):** Assertive. The comma after "Eyes up" is a challenge, not a pause. Speed 1.1 gives it urgency.
- **Cue 2 (Social feeds):** Playful. The "Don't worry, I'm here" should sound like she's doing you a favor.
- **Cue 3 (Error):** Deadpan. No alarm. She's seen worse. Speed 1.1 makes it feel like an automated system message.
- **Cue 4 (Lobster):** **THE critical render.** Speed 1.05 — fast enough to feel like it just tumbled out, slow enough to be legible. The 0.5s pause after "peeing at one another?" must be natural, not mechanical. "Just felt like you should know" should trail off slightly.
- **Cue 5 (Anyway):** Brisk. This is a transition, not a punchline. Speed 1.0, minimal weight.
- **Cue 6 (Face):** Cheerful but not saccharine. "Stare-into-your-soul" should be delivered with a slight edge — the smile doesn't reach the eyes.
- **Cue 7 (Soul):** Completely deadpan. This is the funniest line in the script if delivered straight.
- **Cue 8 (Superbrain):** Speed 1.0. "Eight idiots in a trench coat" is the core — lean into it. The pause before "But we get the job done" should be just long enough to make the viewer wonder if she's done.
- **Cue 9 (Rats):** Genuinely cheerful. The "Freudensprünge" pronunciation should be precise but not performative. Playful rise on "approve this pull request."
- **Cue 10 (Corner):** Speed 0.85, trailing off. Each fragment gets quieter: "Don't mind me" → normal. "I'll just be here" → softer. "In your peripheral vision" → barely above whisper. "Forever" → almost silent.
- **Cue 11 (Callback):** Speed 0.7. Tiny. Satisfied. She's home.

### Expression Mapping (Allowed values: normal, serious, cheerful)
- **cheerful:** Cues 1, 6, 9, 11 — arrival, customization offer, rats+PR, callback
- **normal:** Cues 2, 4, 5, 8, 10 — social feeds, lobster, bridge, superbrain, corner
- **serious:** Cues 3, 7 — error overflow, middle manager

### Lip-Sync Notes
The lip-sync showcase is Cue 8 ("I'm not a superbrain"). This is the only cue flagged for mouth animation sync. Ensure the viseme tracking is calibrated for phonetic precision on "trench coat" — the hard 'ch' and 't' sounds are good visual anchors for sync verification.
